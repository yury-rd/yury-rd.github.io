Engineering-Native Artificial Intelligence

I develop AI that is born from engineering — not applied to it as an afterthought. Research

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning theory and engineering practice — creating AI methods that are structurally shaped by the physical systems they serve.

⚡

Engineering-Native AI

Designing novel neural architectures and optimization algorithms where engineering constraints — symmetries, conservation laws, stability requirements — are not regularizers bolted on after the fact, but the very scaffolding from which models are built.

News

Recently accepted and published papers.

IEEE Access March 2026

B3Emo: Quantifying Affect as a Double-Edged Sword in Strategic LLM Interactions

Systematic study of emotional reasoning in LLM strategic decision-making. Manipulative agents excel in information-asymmetric games, but emotional reasoning becomes counterproductive under high uncertainty. Humans elicited 2x more cooperation from emotionally attuned LLMs.

IEEE Access March 2026

Bridging Global Climate Solutions and Local Realities: Evaluating Neural Networks for High-Resolution Downscaling

Unified open-source framework for neural downscaling of climate data. Transformer-based models achieve highest accuracy; generative models produce realistic fine-scale structure in complex terrain. Nine architectures benchmarked across three public datasets.

Frontiers in Climate March 2026

Neural Network-Based Approach to Evaluate Convective Hazards Frequency Shift under Climate Change

A robust neural network architecture for predicting extreme weather events that outperforms common baselines, leveraging physics encapsulated in CMIP data. Highlights the impact of rugged terrain on extreme weather risk distribution.

AAMAS May 2026

LLM-Guided Multi-Agent Evacuation Coordination via Episodic Memory and Cognitive Task Analysis

A two-level architecture for wildfire evacuation: RL-driven vehicle policies coordinated by an LLM commander that issues network-level routing rules, with a cognitive task analysis layer for human interpretability.

AAAI February 2026

Efficient Contextual Bandit Learning via Reward-Space Sampling and Online Optimization

A new approach combining reward sampling with online stochastic optimization for contextual bandits. Attains near-optimal reward more quickly and with substantially lower computation than Thompson Sampling and UCB.

AAAI February 2026

RESPOND: Realistic Environment Simulation of Population and Natural Disasters with LLM-Driven Agents

A multi-agent LLM-enhanced platform that jointly simulates natural hazards and human response, coupling high-fidelity flood AI forecasting with agent-based models of human behavior for emergency management.

EACL February 2026

Unveiling Intrinsic Dimension of Texts: from Academic Abstract to Creative Story

First comprehensive study grounding intrinsic dimension in interpretable text properties. Scientific prose shows low ID (~8), encyclopedic content medium (~9), and creative writing high (~10.5) — revealing that LLMs find scientific text "representationally simple."
